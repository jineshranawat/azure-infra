Orientation

How to read this programme

This is a build-and-operate course. It assumes a mixed-ability cohort, so it opens with a developer-and-cloud foundations day (Day 0) that levels everyone up, threads a software-engineering lens through the stack days, and closes with a dedicated engineering-excellence module (Days 6–7) on testing, CI/CD and framework design. Throughout, Python is the common control plane — for writing the pipelines and for operating, monitoring and remediating them.

AudienceData engineers and developers — including those new to Azure or to data engineering — who will build and run the platform. Day 0Beginner-friendly ramp: dev environment, Python for data, Git, SQL, cloud & Azure fundamentals. Confident teams can fast-track it. Delivery~35% instruction, ~65% guided labs. Each day ends in an integrated lab; the artefact compounds across the week. OutcomeAble to design, code, test, deploy, govern, cost-control and support an end-to-end Azure ETL platform — to an engineering standard.

Decision

Should every learner use a personal account & card? No — here's the better way

For a regulated, corporate cohort, having each learner spin up a personal Azure account on a personal card is the wrong default: billing is fragmented (so you can't teach or practise central cost monitoring — the whole point of this course), it creates compliance exposure for a central bank, free credits expire on different dates so the cohort drifts, and Databricks Free Edition is for non-commercial use. Choose the environment to fit the goal:

The lens

Non-functional requirements for a central bank

A central bank's platform is judged on trust. These eight NFRs are the recurring lens; every lab is tagged against the ones it exercises, and the trade-offs (CAP/PACELC, cost vs. resilience, latency vs. consistency) are made explicit.

Security & residency
UK region pinning, encryption, private endpoints, least-privilege identity, Key Vault secrets.
Auditability & lineage
Source-to-serve traceability; immutable audit logs; who-changed-what.
Availability & resilience
Zone/region redundancy, graceful degradation, no single points of failure.
Recoverability
Defined RPO/RTO, tested restores, Delta time-travel, control-plane backup.
Data quality & integrity
Validation at ingest, schema enforcement, reconciliation, dead-letter handling.
Observability
Metrics, logs, traces, alerting; mean-time-to-detect over vanity dashboards.
Scalability & performance
Elastic compute, partitioning, predictable throughput under load.
Cost governance
Tagging, budgets, anomaly detection, unit economics per pipeline.
